Overview

You may need a national police certificate as part of the application process for a licence, registration or other approval. Some employers or volunteer organisations may also require you to provide a national police certificate prior to commencing employment or volunteer work.

A National Police Certificate (NPC) conducted by the Queensland Police Service (QPS) is based on a search of the person's name against the criminal history records held by police services Australia-wide. This document is most commonly used for employment, study or overseas travel (check with your employer or licensing body to ensure a NPC is appropriate). In some cases, you may also be required to undergo a fingerprint search or an ASIO security assessment relating to politically motivated violence.

Lodgement process

Apply

To apply for a national police certificate, you must:

  • complete the application form, provide 100 points of identification and pay the prescribed fee
  • reside or work in Queensland.

If your legal name is a genuine mononym (single name) you will not be able to complete the online application and will need to attend an Australia Post outlet to complete your application.

Publications Details process

To view an electronic version of your national police certificate, you must register for a MyPost account and your certificate will be delivered to you via the Australia Post Secure Document Vault. If you have disclosable court outcomes, you will receive your certificate in hard copy via mail to the address specified on your application. Certificates with disclosable histories will not be received online.
