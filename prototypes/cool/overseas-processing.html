  • processes that merely change the form or appearance of a food or ingredient and do not constitute substantial transformation. For example, cleaning, shelling, slicing, freezing, canning, bottling, crumbing, seasoning, marinating, reconstituting, roasting, packing, packaging and re-packaging.
" %} {% include contextual-help-complex-example.html question="What is the meaning of minor processing with non-Australian ingredients added to the food?" answer="

The Country of Origin Food Labelling Standard 2016 does not permit a grown in, produced in or made in Australia claim to be made for a food product that is:

  • substantially transformed in Australia; and
  • then exported to an overseas country for minor processing with non-Australian ingredients added; and
  • then re-imported to Australia.
" %} {% include contextual-help-complex-example.html question="What is the meaning of substantial transformation?" answer="
  • a food is considered to have been substantially transformed if the end product is something fundamentally different from its ingredients. For example, a cake (final product) is made up of ingredients (flour, sugar, eggs, spices, yeast) that are fundamentally different from the finished product.
