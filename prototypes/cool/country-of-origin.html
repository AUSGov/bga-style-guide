Country of origin

Was this food product grown OR produced OR made in Australia?

{% include contextual-help-complex-example.html question="What is the meaning of 'grown in Australia' AND 'produced in Australia'?" answer="

a food or ingredient is considered to be grown OR produced in Australia if it was grown in Australia and virtually all of its processing occurred in Australia. The main difference between ‘produced in’ and ‘grown in’ is that ‘produced’ covers both food that is grown and food that is not grown, for example milk, eggs and water. For more details on this subject refer to our help topics page.

" %} {% include contextual-help-complex-example.html question="What is the meaning of 'made in Australia'?" answer="
  • a food or ingredient is considered to be made in Australia if it underwent its last substantial transformation in Australia.
  • a food is considered to have been substantially transformed if the end product is something fundamentally different from its ingredients. For example, a cake (final product) is made up of ingredients (flour, sugar, eggs, spices, yeast) that are fundamentally different from the finished product. For more details on this subject refer to our help topics page.
  • minor processes that merely change the form or appearance of a food or ingredient such as cleaning, shelling, slicing, freezing, canning, bottling, crumbing, reconstituting, roasting, packing, packaging and re-packaging ARE NOT considered to be substantial transformation.
" %}
