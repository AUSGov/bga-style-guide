Business or hobby

Are you starting (or already running) a business?

Before you apply for business registrations make sure you will be running a business. Not everything you do to make money is a business.

It's important to understand the characteristics of businesses and hobbies to ensure you correctly determine what your activities are.

Differences between a business and a hobby

There is no single factor that determines if you are in business. Compare the list below to help you decide if you are running a business or a hobby.

Consideration Hobby Business
Profit: You do not make a profit. You intend to make a profit.

Generally you are conducting a hobby if you gift or sell your work for the cost of materials.

You are running a business if You intend to make a profit – or genuinely believe you will make a profit from the activity – even if you are unlikely to do so in the short term.
Activity frequency: You do activities in your own time or when people contact you You repeat similar types of activities
