Business name

Will you use your own name as your business name?

You need to register your business name if it’s different to your own name. For example, if your name is Joe Aaron Smith, and you want to use it (or a shortened form such as Joe Smith, J Smith, or J. A. Smith) as your business name, you don’t need to register it.

You need to register a business name if it's anything else ('Joe Smith Plumbing').

Do you want to protect your business name so it is unique to your business?

Registering your business name doesn't stop other businesses from using it for their products or services. You can protect your business name by applying for a trade mark.

Do you plan to set up a business website?

Website addresses are called domain names, for example, sarahsflowers.com.au or josephsmitharchitects.com.au.

If you're keen to have a domain name that reflects your business name, it's a good idea to register your domain name even if you're not quite ready to set up your website.
