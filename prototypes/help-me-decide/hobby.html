A hobby is a pastime or leisure activity conducted in your spare time for recreation or pleasure.

Having a hobby allows you to:

  • gain personal enjoyment and satisfaction from the activity
  • gift or sell your work for the cost of materials
  • do it in your own time or when people contact you
  • have no reporting obligations of a business.

Key facts about hobbies

  • Declaring payments - You do not need to declare the amounts you make from your hobby to the Australian Taxation Office (ATO).
  • Claiming tax deductions - You cannot claim a deduction for any losses from your creative work if it is a hobby.
  • Keeping records - records You do not need to keep records of your hobby for the ATO, however it’s good practice to keep records in case your circumstances change.
  • Licences and permits - Generally, you will not need to hold licences and permits for your hobby.
  • Australian Business Number (ABN) eligibility - You are not eligible for an ABN for a hobby, however if you sell goods or services to businesses, they may ask you for an ABN when they pay you. You can use a Statement by a supplier form to avoid the business withholding an amount from the payment to you for not having an ABN. The statement lets the business know that you are selling the goods or services as your hobby.
  • Selling goods - If you’re selling goods, you’ll need to comply with Australian Consumer Law (ACL). Your customers have automatic rights if they buy a product that breaks easily, doesn’t work or doesn’t perform as generally expected. Read more about fair trading.
