Major Event Liquor Licence - Victoria

You need this licence if you are organising a major event and you or your vendors want to sell alcohol at the event.

A major event is defined as one where:

  • a large crowd will be in attendance (5000 people)
  • it involves significant coordination from Victoria Police, local council, public transport or emergency services.

Please note, this licence is not a permit to hold your event. You may need a permit from the relevant local council.

Liquor Licensing Exemptions - Victoria

A business is required to hold a liquor licence if it sells alcohol to its customers or guests. However, certain types of businesses are exempt from this requirement, provided that certain conditions are met.

These exemptions may apply to the following types of businesses:

  • bed and breakfast businesses
  • florists
  • gift-makers
  • hairdressers
  • butchers
  • hospitals
  • residential care services
  • retirement villages
  • cruise ships.

Conditions vary for each type of business. For example, the operator of a bed and breakfast may supply only 750mL of alcohol per day per room occupied. A butcher may only supply 1.5 litres of alcohol to a customer in a day.

Permit to Provide Liquor in Public Places - Victoria

You will need this permit if you intend to control the consumption of liquor in a public place. This refers to control of the sale, possession and consumption of liquor in an unsealed container.

Places covered by this permit may vary and include all those owned, occupied, managed or cared for by your local council to which the public has access (regardless of whether there is an admittance fee, such as for a special public celebration, event or festival). This also includes within a motor vehicle that is within or on a public place.

Alcohol supply restrictions exist on ANZAC Day between 3am and 12pm.

Your council may stipulate certain areas and times for which this permit is required. You may also require liquor licensing at a State government level.

Pre-Retail Licence - Victoria

You will need this licence if you intend to supply liquor by wholesale.

A pre-retail licence authorises you to:

  • supply liquor to any liquor licence holder
  • supply liquor to persons authorised to sell or supply liquor interstate for consumption outside Victoria
  • supply liquor to liquor licence holders for the purposes of export.

Please note, this licence does not authorise retail sale of liquor or sale to members of the public who are not liquor licensees.

Renewable Limited Club Licence - Victoria

You will need this licence if you intend to supply alcohol in a limited or restricted way.

Renewable limited licence holders tend to fall outside other types of liquor licences, and include:

  • off-site caterers
  • motels and accommodation hotels, for their mini-bars
  • caravan parks
  • bed and breakfasts
  • other small businesses where the supply of liquor is ancillary to their business that do not fit within the exemption categories below
  • clubs (clubs have their own renewable liquor licence, see the information below).

Restricted Club Licence - Victoria

You will need this licence if you operate a club and intend to supply alcohol. A restricted club licence authorises you to supply alcohol on your premises to:

  • club members to drink on the premises
  • guests of members to drink on the premises only
  • people attending a function or club event for drinking on the premises.
