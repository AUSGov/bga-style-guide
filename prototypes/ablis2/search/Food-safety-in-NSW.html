Approval as a Food Safety Auditor - New South Wales

You will need this approval if you intend to act as a food safety auditor. Food safety auditors have the power to:

  • carry out audits of businesses' food safety programs
  • carry out follow-up actions as a result of audits
  • carry out assessments of businesses' compliance with the Food Safety Standards
  • report on the results of your audits to the Authority.

Approval to Issue Food Safety Supervisor Certificates - New South Wales

You will need this approval if you operate a registered training organisation (RTO) that intends to issue food safety supervisor (FSS) certificates.

Please note that you must ensure that your RTO does not allow any person to conduct training related to the FSS certificate unless they have been approved to do so in writing by the Authority.

All businesses that deal with potentially hazardous, ready-to eat food that is not pre-packaged are required to appoint at least one FSS. The types of food businesses that may need to have a FSS are restaurants, cafes, takeaway shops, caterers, bakeries, pubs, clubs, hotels and supermarkets (hot food sales).

Food Delivery Work Health and Safety Requirements - New South Wales

You will need to comply with these requirements if you are a food delivery booking provider.

Food delivery booking providers (platforms) must provide food delivery riders with:

  • high-visibility personal protective equipment (PPE), including a retroreflective outer clothing item and a bag or container for safely transporting food or drink. The garment and delivery bag must comply with the relevant Australian Standard
  • induction training which covers hazard and fatigue management, road safety, use of PPE and WHS duties and obligations.

A food delivery rider means a person engaged by a food delivery provider to deliver food or drink and who delivers the food or drink using a bicycle or motor bike.

Platforms:

  • do not need to provide PPE if the rider is able to use PPE already provided by another platform
  • must maintain records of the provision of PPE and induction training
  • provide each rider with a training record which verifies that the rider has successfully completed induction training.

Vulnerable Persons Food Business Licence - New South Wales

You will need this licence if you intend to operate a business that processes or serves food to vulnerable people. A vulnerable person for the purpose of this licence is any person with a vulnerable immune system, such as:

  • the elderly
  • children under four years of age
  • pregnant women
  • people receiving therapeutic care / medical assistance in an identified facility.

Businesses which may require this licence include:

  • hospitals
  • aged care facilities and care services
  • respite services
  • certain delivered meal organisations.

Please note that:

  • all businesses that supply food to vulnerable people must comply with relevant parts of the Food Standards Code
  • depending on the nature of your activities you may need to have an audited Food Safety Program in place for your business
  • some businesses may be exempt from this licence.

National Livestock Identification Scheme (NLIS) - Australian Government

You will be required to comply with the National Livestock Identification System (NLIS) if you are a producer, saleyard, processor or have livestock under your control. You will require registration for all livestock under your control and you must record their movements.

The NLIS is Australia's system for the identification and tracing of cattle, goats, lambs and sheep. It is a permanent whole-of-life identification system that tracks individual animals from property of birth to slaughter for food safety, product integrity and market access purposes.

Plant Products Licence - New South Wales

You will need this licence if you intend to operate a business that will be involved in any of the following activities involving high risk plant products:

  • processing of fruit or vegetables to produce fresh cut ready-to-eat products
  • sprouting and processing of seed spouts
  • extraction and / or packaging of juice from fruit or vegetables where the juice is not subsequently pasteurised
  • production of vegetables or fruit in oil
  • refrigerated storage of these plant products
  • refrigerated distribution and / or transporting of these plant products.

Please note that:

  • all businesses that supply high risk plant products must comply with relevant parts of the Food Standards Code
  • depending on the nature of your activities you may need to have an audited Food Safety Program in place for your business.

Seafood Licence - New South Wales

You will need this licence if you intend to operate a seafood business that includes undertaking activities such as harvesting, catching, processing, packaging, storing, transporting and wholesaling seafood.

Please note that:

  • this licence does not apply to the retail sale of seafood
  • if you operate a business involving shellfish you will require a different licence. For instance bivalve molluscs (for example, cockles, clams, mussels, oysters, pipis and scallops) for cultivation and / harvest
  • all seafood product businesses must comply with relevant parts of the Food Standards Code
  • depending on the nature of your activities you may need to have an audited Food Safety Program in place for your business.

Licence to Process or Store Meat Products - New South Wales

You will need this licence if you intend to operate a business that processes, stores or handles meat. This includes businesses such as:

  • retail meat premises
  • meat vans
  • game meat harvesters' vehicles and field chillers (for animal food or human consumption)
  • meat processing plants
  • knackeries and rendering plants
  • domestic and export abattoirs.

Please note that:

  • this includes businesses that deal in a range of different animal meat (for example: red meat, poultry, rabbit, game meat, etc) and that there are specific rules for different types of meat
  • all meat product businesses must comply with relevant parts of the Food Standards Code
  • depending on the nature of your activities you may need to have an audited Food Safety Program in place for your business.
