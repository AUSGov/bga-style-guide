Style rules

The illustration style for business.gov.au is simple and refined. Illustrations help lighten the tone of the site and make the pages feel more colourful and friendly.

Illustrations tell a positive story, rather than using negative or emotive imagery.

The following style rules are used to ensure a consistent look and feel across the site:

  • Illustrations are based on the https://www.humaaans.com/ illustration library.
  • Only colours in the BGA palette are used in the illustrations (see below for colour use).
  • Illustrations consist of three layers, background lines (2pt thickness), background fill and primary illustration.
    • The background lines are always ‘Teal highlight - dark’ (#5AB3B3)
    • The background fill is always ‘Teal - illustration background’ (#BFE2E2)
    • The primary illustration is made up of colours from the BGA highlight colours (excluding teal) and the blue black tones.
  • Line weights should scale with the illustration when displayed at a larger size.
  • Lines have rounded corners and endpoints.
  • Illustrations should not be too complicated, a few simple lines is preferred over a high-level of detail.
  • Illustrations should only display objects or situations that belong together. The background surrounding the core illustration should depict a landscape or objects that would naturally be seen with the core subject matter.
  • When people are included they do not have faces.
  • Words should not be included in illustrations unless they are part of the picture.
