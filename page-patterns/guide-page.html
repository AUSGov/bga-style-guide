Page anatomy

Each guide consists of a single landing page with a series of numbered accordions that open to reveal pathway tiles linking to BGA information pages.

Accordions with numbered steps. They should contain:

  • A unique heading.
  • Short descriptions with only a few sentences which highlight the accordions content.
  • An image with colours matching the accordion background colour.
" item5="Pathway accordion tile" content5="

A list of pathway tiles. Tiles should:

  • Not be numbered.
  • Be tagged as recommended or required.
  • Be ordered based on tag type, required tiles listed first, then recommended.
  • Contain an action oriented title.
  • Contain a short description which is contextual to the guide content.
  • Contain labels reflecting the title of the linked page.

Guidelines for use

Purpose

Guides on BGA are a navigation pattern that pull together information from different areas of the site. They link to site content, not external content.

General rules:

  • Guides should have a clearly defined purpose for a specific audience.
  • Guides often address a particular business scenario/business life event. For example starting a business or closing a business.
  • Guides present information that is intended to be read sequentially (i.e. a step by step process).
  • If establishing a new guide, all content pages need to follow BGA content principles.
  • New guide pages should not duplicate information already in current business.gov.au content.
  • Guides should aim to contain no more than 20 BGA information pages. If guides are much larger than 20 pages, consider breaking them into smaller journeys.
  • Guide tiles should only lead to information pages, not further navigational pages. (A possible exception may be linking to information that differs for different individuals in the intended audience, e.g. linking to industry specific legal information).
